Explore the Neighbourhood

Discover what awaits beyond the hillside

Ahana Hillside sits in the green hills just outside Cairns, the gateway to Tropical North Queensland. From here, two UNESCO World Heritage sites are at your doorstep — the Great Barrier Reef and the Daintree Rainforest — along with hidden waterfalls, volcanic crater lakes, and some of Australia's most scenic drives.

Whether you're here for adventure or simply to soak in the surroundings, there's something remarkable in every direction.

What's Nearby

Explore the best of Tropical North Queensland, all within easy reach of Ahana Hillside

World Heritage

Great Barrier Reef

The world's largest coral reef system stretches over 2,300 kilometres along the Queensland coast. From Cairns, day trips take you to the outer reef for snorkelling, diving, and glass-bottom boat tours among vibrant coral gardens and marine life. Whether it's your first time or your fiftieth, the reef never fails to amaze.

Day trips depart daily from Cairns Marina · ~45 min by boat

Ancient Rainforest

Daintree Rainforest

At over 180 million years old, the Daintree is the oldest continuously surviving tropical rainforest on Earth. Drive north from Cairns along one of the world's great coastal roads, cross the river by cable ferry, and step into a primeval world of towering fan palms, crystal-clear swimming holes, and dense canopy walks. Cape Tribulation, where the rainforest meets the reef, is unforgettable.

~2 hours drive north of Ahana Hillside

Natural Wonders

Waterfalls & Swimming Holes

Tropical North Queensland is home to some of the most beautiful waterfalls in Australia. Crystal Cascades is a local favourite just minutes from the city — a series of pristine pools surrounded by lush rainforest. Further afield, Josephine Falls tumbles over enormous granite boulders (with natural rock slides), Babinda Boulders offers dramatic rainforest swimming, and Millaa Millaa Falls is the picture-perfect curtain waterfall you've seen on postcards.

Crystal Cascades ~20 min · Josephine Falls ~1 hr · Babinda Boulders ~50 min

Highlands

Atherton Tablelands

Rising behind Cairns, the Atherton Tablelands are a patchwork of rolling green hills, volcanic crater lakes, and rainforest pockets. Visit Lake Eacham and Lake Barrine for tranquil swims in volcanic lakes, explore the giant curtain fig tree, spot platypus at Yungaburra, or visit the Herberton Historic Village. The cooler highland climate makes it a refreshing day out.

~1 hour drive from Ahana Hillside via Gillies Highway

Coastal

Beaches & Islands

While Cairns itself sits on a mud-flat estuary, stunning beaches are just a short drive away. Palm Cove offers a palm-lined esplanade with calm waters, Trinity Beach is a family favourite, and the northern beaches stretch all the way to Port Douglas. For a true tropical island experience, take a boat to Fitzroy Island or Green Island — both offer snorkelling right off the beach.

Palm Cove ~25 min · Fitzroy Island ~45 min by ferry

The City

Cairns City

Cairns is a relaxed, tropical city with a vibrant waterfront. The Esplanade Lagoon offers free saltwater swimming in the heart of the city, surrounded by barbecue areas and parklands. Explore Rusty's Markets for tropical fruit and local produce on weekends, dine at the waterfront restaurants, or take a stroll along the Boardwalk through mangroves and mudflats where you can spot crocodiles and birdlife.

~35 min drive from Ahana Hillside

Quick Guide

🚗

Getting Here

Cairns Airport is just 35 minutes away by car, with domestic and international flights. A rental car is recommended to explore the region freely — most attractions are within a 1–2 hour drive.

☀

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May–October) offers warm days and cool evenings. The wet season (November–April) is hotter and more humid with tropical downpours, but the landscape is at its most lush.

🦙

Wildlife

Keep an eye out for cassowaries, tree kangaroos, platypus, and an incredible variety of tropical birds. Estuarine crocodiles inhabit local waterways — always swim in designated areas.

🌊

Stinger Season

Marine stingers (jellyfish) are present in coastal waters from November to May. Swim within stinger nets at beaches, or wear a stinger suit when on reef trips.

🍍

Local Produce

The region is known for tropical fruits, coffee, chocolate, and macadamias. Visit Rusty's Markets, Gallo Dairyland, and the many farm gates along the Tablelands.

🏄

Adventure Activities

Beyond reef and rainforest: bungee jumping, white-water rafting on the Tully and Barron rivers, hot air ballooning over the Tablelands, and the Skyrail Rainforest Cableway.
